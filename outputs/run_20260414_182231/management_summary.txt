PG3 INVESTMENT COMMITTEE SUMMARY

1. Overall assessment
The PG3 case is conditionally investable. While base-case returns are acceptable, the limited valuation buffer and sensitivity to key assumptions require disciplined structuring and pricing.

2. Base-case valuation
Under the 10% base case, the model indicates an IRR of 10.48%, a MOIC of 2.82x, and an expected NPV of 4.65.

3. Downside risk
The probability of a negative NPV is 33.74%, while NPV CVaR (5%) is -14.05.
Downside risk is material: the probability of adverse valuation outcomes is elevated, indicating a limited valuation buffer under current assumptions.

4. Discount-rate sensitivity
At a 9% discount rate, expected NPV is 13.79; at 10%, 4.65; and at 11%, -3.45.
Valuation is highly sensitive to discount rate assumptions, reflecting the long-duration nature of the cashflows. The investment case transitions from clearly positive to negative within a narrow range of required returns.

5. Macro / scenario context
The strongest macro scenario in the current framework is 'Upside' with an expected NPV of 16.27.
The weakest macro scenario is 'High_Rate' with an expected NPV of -13.88.
This confirms that valuation is not only rate-sensitive but also exposed to valuation compression and cashflow uncertainty.

6. Contract intelligence
In the deterministic contract comparison, the structured royalty changes NPV by -6.63 and total payout by -1.10 versus the simple royalty benchmark.
In the stylised Monte Carlo contract comparison, the structured royalty changes expected NPV by -5.69 and CVaR by -3.57.
The contract comparison shows that structured royalty mechanics can reduce present value relative to a simple flat royalty, primarily due to payment lag, caps, and altered payout timing.

7. Investment conclusion
Final decision: INVEST WITH CONDITIONS.
Risk flag: MODERATE DOWNSIDE RISK.

Overall, the case offers acceptable base-case returns but remains exposed to material downside risk and valuation sensitivity.
Investment is justified only under disciplined entry conditions, appropriate pricing, and a clear understanding of the underlying risk profile.
